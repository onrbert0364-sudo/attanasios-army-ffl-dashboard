Chip Deployment Guide

🃏 Wildcard

Use when squad has ≥3 premium assets to replace, or before a run of fixtures where the current squad is poorly positioned. Activating before a DGW round maximises value. Do not waste on single-position fixes.

⭐ Triple Captain

Maximum value on a DGW with your captain scoring 2+ fixtures. Target H (Home) fixture in DGW with easy FDR. Engine captain score >80 recommended before using TC.

🚀 Bench Boost

Save for a DGW where your bench has ≥3 players with fixtures. Bench average needs to be strong — a typical bench in a non-DGW averages ~4–6 pts; in a DGW this can be 15–25 pts.

🔄 Free Hit

Best used in a BGW to field 11 players with fixtures. Squad reverts after the GW — plan temporary XI around that week's fixtures only, ignoring long-term value.

GW Planner Notes

World Cup 2026 fatigue note (GW1–3): Players with deep tournament runs (semi-final or final) return with as little as 4 weeks before PL GW1. England, France, Brazil, Argentina, Spain, Germany players are highest risk for rotation or reduced minutes in GW1. Consider FH in GW1 if your squad is heavily WC-fatigued and you don't want to burn a Wildcard.

Weight Rationale

Captain: Form (0.30) and xG (0.25) dominate — we need ceiling, not consistency. Minutes weight is near-zero because premium captains almost always start.

Transfer: Fixture run (0.32) is the dominant factor across a 3-GW horizon. Buying a player into a blank costs more than any form difference.

Bench: Minutes reliability (0.48) dominates — we need players who will play, regardless of quality.

Starting XI: Balanced across form (0.25), xG (0.20), and fixture (0.22). More conservative than captain.
